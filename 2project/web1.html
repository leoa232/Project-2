Background

  • 1960s - Cold war between the Soviet Union and the USA.
  • The Internet started as a project to allow the US to launch a counter nuclear strike after a war had started.
  • The Internet was designed to take many hits and remain functional.
  • 1964 - RAND Corporation made a new type of network. Paul Baran made a proposal
    • indicating that the new network would have no central authority.
  • The principles of this network were: All the nodes would be equal in status, each could send and receive messages.
  • 1968- The first test network based on these ideas was installed in The National Research Laboratory in Great Britain in. Shortly afterwards, the Pentagon's Advanced Research Projects Agency (ARPA) wanted to install a more advanced network based on the same principles in the USA.
